What is climate?


Climate refers to the long-term patterns of temperature, humidity, wind, and precipitation in a particular region or on Earth as a whole. It is distinct from weather, which describes short-term atmospheric conditions over a specific period of time.

Climate Conditions Worldwide

Climate change is causing many problems for humans and wildlife, including:

  • Health: Climate change can cause air pollution, disease, and increased hunger and poor nutrition. It can also lead to mental health issues like anxiety and post-traumatic stress.
  • Weather: Climate change is causing more frequent and intense droughts, storms, heat waves, and rising sea levels. In the United States, people are facing heat waves, wildfires, coastal storms, and flooding.
  • Food: Climate change is affecting food availability, quality, and diversity, which can exacerbate food and nutrition crises.
  • Animals: Climate change can harm animals, destroy their habitats, and wreak havoc on their livelihoods and communities.
  • Oceans: Climate change is causing ocean acidification, which is a change in seawater chemistry caused by the ocean absorbing around one third of all human-induced carbon dioxide.

Types of Pollution

Air Pollution

Air pollution is the presence of harmful substances in the air, such as particulate matter, nitrogen dioxide, and sulfur dioxide. It can lead to respiratory problems, cardiovascular diseases, and other health issues.

Water Pollution

Water pollution occurs when contaminants are introduced into water bodies, such as rivers, lakes, and oceans. It can negatively impact aquatic life, human health, and the environment.

Soil Pollution

Soil pollution involves the contamination of soil with harmful chemicals and substances, such as pesticides, heavy metals, and industrial waste. It can degrade soil quality and affect plant growth and food safety.

Noise Pollution

Noise pollution is excessive or disruptive noise that can interfere with human activities and cause annoyance, stress, and hearing loss. Common sources of noise pollution include traffic, industrial machinery, and loud music.

Air Pollution in India

India is one of the most polluted countries in the world, with air pollution levels exceeding safe limits in many cities. The Air Quality Index (AQI) is a measure used to assess air quality, with higher values indicating higher levels of pollution. Despite efforts to reduce pollution, India continues to face significant challenges in improving air quality and protecting public health.
